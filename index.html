In Manhattan's Chinatown, Opposing Views of Gentrification

Chinatown residents torn over new luxury developments in the area.

by Samantha Lee

Ya Qin Li, a longtime tenant of a mixed-use building on Bowery Street in Manhattan's Chinatown, has been dealing with water leaks, an irregular gas supply and gradually worsening living conditions for several years now.

Her building, along with nine others on the same street, was sold to a developer for $62 million in 2013. Since then, her landlord has allowed the building to fall into disrepair, she said at a recent rally protesting the construction of a luxury condominium on Cherry Street.

"The developer also started to kick some of us out; he wants to take down our buildings and build luxury high rises," she said. "We are being displaced by landlords and developers. This is not something I can afford."
